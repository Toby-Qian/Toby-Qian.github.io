Notes from life in Guangzhou.

Weather, late-night snacks, small things worth remembering — a season's worth of margins.

By Toby Qian May 2026 Guangzhou

Guangzhou in May is a city that sweats politely. The rain comes in short, exact bursts at three in the afternoon, and everyone on the metro stares at their phone with the same small smile, as if they'd been let in on the joke.

I've been keeping a list of things I want to write down before they fade. The way the egg waffle lady at the BRT stop wraps each one in a paper bag and folds the top twice. The light in the corridor of my building at 6:30 a.m. The exact pitch of the elevator beep.

A short inventory

Iced lemon tea from the corner shop, no sugar. Cha can ting at the end of the street that opens at 7 and closes when it feels like. A river that's never quite the same color twice. The kind of friends you only see when you both happen to be walking home along the same lane.

Cities reveal themselves at the edges — the unswept staircase, the half-broken sign, the convenience store at 2 a.m.

This entry is short on purpose. The point of writing it down at all is so I'll remember the texture of right now when right now is gone.
