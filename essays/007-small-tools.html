On building small tools.

Why one-page utilities still beat the fanciest framework — and what they teach you about taste.

By Toby Qian Published 11 May 2026 Guangzhou

A small tool is a tool that does one thing in one screen and trusts you to bring everything else. It loads in under a second. It doesn't ask you to sign in. You don't need a tutorial. You drop something in, you get something out, and the friction between idea and result is roughly the same as the friction between thinking and speaking.

Most of what I make sits in this shape. Not because I can't build bigger — but because the small shape is the one that gets used.

What I keep learning

The interesting part of a small tool is never the algorithm. It is the decision of which six controls survive and which fifty get cut. The taste is in the cutting.

Every feature is a cost. The good ones earn it back. The rest are interest payments forever.

That is most of what I'd want a younger version of myself to know. Make it small. Ship it. Use it. Throw out half. Make it again.
